Achievements


2014,2015,2016

Received the Great Non-Profits Seal for being one of thetop rated NGO's for these years. The Great Non-profits seal is the second most recognized.

2016

Launched the Solidarity Gatherings which are mostly gatherings to bring national awareness to the different forms of violence which continues to undermine the cocio-economic health and wellbeing of individuals, families and communities and to encourage citizens to become active changemakers and unite towards the process of change, reduction of crime and for the sustainable development for our country.

2016
Partnered with the Gender and Child Affairs Unit for the CARE Packages Drive. OABI collected over 70 care packages which were handed to the Gender and Child Affairs Unit for distribution to residents of various domestic violence centers.

2021
Domestic violence survivor, activist, founder and president of the Organisatio n for Abused and Battered Individuals Sherna Alexander-Benjamin recently received the President Volunteer Service Award (Gold), via AmeriCorps in recognition of her volunteer service in the areas of climate, environmental, and gender justice, working with US-based NGO Atlantic Climate Justice Alliance (ACJA), founded by Maria Santiago-Valentin.

Our Services

  • Advocate at all levels for the prevention, reduction and elimination of all formns of dicrimination and gender-based violence against women and girls. All levels means, individual,community, institutional, legislative and regional.
  • Provide community outreach to bring awareness and to educate community residents about gender-equity and various forms of gender-based violence.
  • Our legal team provides advice to clients and their familes and work with them through the reporting and judiciary process.
  • Offer individual and family support and counselling. (Psychosocial and therapuetic services)
  • Host safe group meetings for victim support and break out sessions.
  • Digital Empowerment of women and girls.
  • Provide training and workshop courses in different areas. (for example, victim advocacy, conflict resolution and therapeutic mediation, social and emotional intelligence, human capicity development)
  • Provide life enhancement and empoerment sessions for women and girls to become self-sustainable and independent.
  • Capcity development workshops for external organizations and our own organization.
  • Assist with Research
  • Youth Development and Mentoring
  • Client referral
  • Poverty alleviation projects
  • Sourcing of safe houses and shelters for victims.
  • Embarking on organization networking, collaboration and partnerships

Gender and Child Affairs

Victims of abuse are at risk of becoming ensnared in a cycle of violence and even of becoming abusers themselves. We all have a responsibility to fulfill children's rights to ensure that the lives of boys and girls are free from abuse, neglect and violence. Silence is acceptance.

PROSAF of St Lucia

Surviving Sexual Abuse in the Caribbean is specifically responsible for creating a positive and supportive environment for speaking out against sexual assualt and other forms of sexual abuse in St. Lucia and the wider Caribbean. It aims to draw attention to the various issues that are related to living through various types of sexual violence and to provide support and resources for survivors in St. Lucia and the wider Caribben.
